Market Runner Platform
3/19

Problem Statement

Despite the popularity of traditional markets in Zanzibar, accessing fresh produce remains challenging for many residents and visitors.

  • Access Issues: Busy professionals lack time, elderly residents face mobility challenges
  • Knowledge Gap: Tourists and expats are unfamiliar with local markets and vendors
  • Infrastructure Limitations: Traditional markets remain essential but lack modern connectivity
  • Service Gap: Limited delivery options from local vendors creates unmet demand
  • Economic Impact: Vendors lose potential customers due to accessibility barriers
Market Runner Platform
4/19

Our Solution: Market Runner Platform

A community-driven digital marketplace that connects traditional market vendors with customers through local shoppers ("runners")

Key Features:

  • Mobile-friendly ordering platform
  • Verified local runner network
  • Real-time order tracking
  • Secure payment options

Benefits:

  • Preserves market culture
  • Creates local jobs
  • Increases market accessibility
  • Supports local vendors

How It Works

1
Order Placement

Customer submits shopping request through website/app

2
Runner Assignment

Local runner accepts the order via platform

3
Market Purchase

Runner buys items from traditional market vendors

4
Delivery

Runner delivers groceries to customer's doorstep

5
Payment

Customer pays through the secure platform

6
Distribution

Platform distributes payment to runner and vendors

Competitor Analysis

Direct Competitors:

  • ZanziEats - Restaurant delivery only, higher fees (25-30%)
  • SwiftMart - Supermarket delivery, limited market reach
  • TourShop - Tourist-focused shopping assistants, premium pricing
  • Traditional Delivery Services - Inconsistent quality, no digital platform

Indirect Competitors:

  • Self-Shopping - Time-consuming, requires market knowledge
  • Hotel Concierges - Limited to tourists, expensive markup
  • Family/Friends - Inconsistent availability, social obligation

Our Competitive Advantages:

  • Hyper-Local Focus: Deep understanding of Zanzibar markets
  • Community Integration: Supporting existing market structures
  • Pricing Model: 10-15% vs 25-30% competitor fees
  • Authenticity: Preserving cultural shopping experience
  • Local Network: Established vendor relationships

Competitive Strategy:

  • Focus on authentic market experience vs. supermarket convenience
  • Emphasize community impact and local economic benefits
  • Offer better value through lower fees and higher quality
  • Build network effects through runner and vendor relationships
